FAQ

We're Always There For Your Help

What are the different types of headphones?

There are several types of headphones available, including over-ear headphones, on-ear headphones, in-ear headphones (earbuds), and wireless headphones. Each type offers different designs, features, and sound experiences.

What is the difference between open-back and closed-back headphones?

Open-back headphones have perforated ear cups that allow air and sound to pass through, providing a more spacious and natural soundstage. Closed-back headphones have sealed ear cups that offer better noise isolation and often more powerful bass response.

What is the difference between active noise cancellation (ANC) and passive noise isolation?

Active noise cancellation uses microphones and circuitry to actively cancel out external noise, providing a quieter listening experience. Passive noise isolation relies on the physical design of the headphones, such as padding and ear cup seal, to block out external noise.

Are wireless headphones as good as wired ones in terms of sound quality?

The sound quality of wireless headphones has improved significantly in recent years. While wired headphones generally offer a more consistent and potentially higher quality audio signal, many wireless headphones now support high-quality audio codecs and provide excellent sound performance.

How can I ensure the longevity of my headphones?

To prolong the lifespan of your headphones, handle them with care, avoid excessive bending or pulling of cables, and store them in a safe and dry place when not in use. Regularly clean the headphones and replace any worn-out ear pads or cables as needed.
